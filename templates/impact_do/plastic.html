Environmental Impact

Plastic can persist for hundreds of years, breaking down into microplastics that contaminate ecosystems and even enter our food and water. Proper recycling and waste management are crucial to mitigate these effects. By choosing recyclable plastics and supporting closed-loop systems, we can reduce plastic pollution and its harmful impact on the environment.
Microplastics are now found in oceans, rivers, air, and even table salt
Threat to wildlife: ingestion and entanglement
Human health risks: possible hormonal disruption, cellular damage, immune system effects
Climate impact: plastic production and incineration release high greenhouse gas emissions

What can we do?

We can reduce plastic pollution by choosing recyclable plastics, supporting closed-loop systems, and making proper waste management decisions.
