Environmental Impact

Cardboard is biodegradable and generally more environmentally friendly than plastic packaging. However, when cardboard ends up in landfills without proper oxygen circulation, it decomposes anaerobically, producing methaneâ€”a potent greenhouse gas. The paper industry is also resource-intensive, requiring significant wood, water, and energy inputs. When properly recycled, cardboard reduces deforestation pressure and uses 75% less energy than producing new cardboard.
Deforestation: cardboard production contributes to forest loss when not sourced sustainably
Water usage: manufacturing requires significant water resources and can cause water pollution
Landfill impact: when not recycled, cardboard takes up valuable landfill space
Chemical treatments: some cardboard contains waxes, dyes, or other treatments that affect recyclability

What can we do?

We can maximize cardboard recycling by flattening boxes, keeping cardboard dry and clean, and removing non-paper elements like tape and staples. Choosing products with minimal packaging and reusing cardboard boxes whenever possible also reduces waste.

Cardboard can be repurposed into furniture, storage solutions, children's toys, and garden seed starters. Corrugated cardboard makes excellent compost material when shredded and can be used as garden mulch. By finding creative ways to reuse cardboard, we extend its lifecycle and prevent the need for new resources.
