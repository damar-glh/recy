Environmental Impact

Paper production contributes to deforestation, biodiversity loss, and water pollution. The paper industry is the third largest industrial emitter of greenhouse gases globally and uses vast amounts of water in manufacturing processes. When paper decomposes in landfills, it produces methane. However, paper is biodegradable and recyclable, making proper disposal crucial for minimizing environmental impact.
Deforestation: paper production is a major driver of forest loss in some regions
Water usage: paper manufacturing requires 10 liters of water per A4 sheet on average
Chemical pollution: pulping and bleaching processes release chlorine compounds and other toxins
Energy consumption: the pulp and paper industry is the fifth largest consumer of energy globally

What can we do?

We can reduce paper waste by going digital when possible, printing on both sides, reusing single-sided paper, and ensuring proper recycling. Choosing recycled paper products and those certified by sustainable forestry programs also helps minimize environmental impact.

Used paper can be transformed into handmade recycled paper, origami art, papier-mâché crafts, and decorative items. Newspaper can be used for seed starters, compost material, or eco-friendly gift wrap. By finding creative ways to reuse paper before recycling, we extend its useful life and reduce the demand for virgin paper products.
