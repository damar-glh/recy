Environmental Impact

Glass is inert and doesn't release harmful chemicals into the environment. However, glass manufacturing is energy-intensive, and when improperly disposed of, glass persists indefinitely in landfills. Glass takes over a million years to decompose naturally. When recycled, glass saves significant energy compared to producing new glass from raw materials and can be recycled infinitely without loss of quality.
Energy use: manufacturing glass requires high temperatures and significant energy
Resource extraction: mining sand, limestone, and other raw materials causes habitat disruption
Transportation emissions: glass is heavy, requiring more fuel to transport
Safety hazards: broken glass can injure wildlife and contaminate other recyclables

What can we do?

We can minimize glass waste by reusing glass containers for storage, choosing products in glass over plastic when appropriate, and ensuring proper recycling by separating glass by color when required.
