Plastic is a versatile, durable, and widely used material, commonly found in packaging, containers, electronics, and household items. Despite its convenience, plastic poses a serious environmental challenge due to its non-biodegradable nature. Improper disposal leads to plastic accumulating in landfills and oceans, where it can persist for hundreds of years. Over time, it breaks down into microplastics, which pose risks to ecosystems and can enter the human food chain. Common types: PET (#1), HDPE (#2), PVC (#3), LDPE (#4), PP (#5), PS (#6), and others
Strengths: strong, cheap, water-resistant
Major concern: non-biodegradable and often not properly recycled
Best practice: choose plastics with higher recyclability and support closed-loop recycling
