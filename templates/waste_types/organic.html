Organic waste comprises biodegradable materials derived from plant or animal sources, including food scraps, yard trimmings, agricultural residues, and other compostable materials. Unlike synthetic waste, organic waste naturally decomposes through biological processes, returning valuable nutrients to the soil. When improperly disposed of in landfills, organic waste produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas contributing to climate change. However, when composted, it creates nutrient-rich soil amendments that improve soil health and reduce the need for chemical fertilizers. Composting organic waste diverts a significant portion of municipal solid waste from landfills, reducing environmental impact while creating a valuable resource for gardens and agriculture.
Common types: food scraps, yard waste, agricultural residues, compostable paper, natural fibers
Strengths: naturally biodegradable, can be converted into valuable compost
Major concern: produces methane in landfills, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions
Best practice: home composting, separating from other waste streams, supporting municipal composting programs
