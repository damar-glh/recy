Paper is a versatile and widely used material made from wood pulp fibers, commonly found in books, newspapers, magazines, office documents, and packaging materials. Paper waste is biodegradable and highly recyclable, making it an environmentally friendly option when properly managed. However, paper production contributes to deforestation and requires significant water and energy resources. When paper ends up in landfills, it decomposes and produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas. Recycling paper saves trees, reduces water pollution, and uses less energy than creating new paper products. Each ton of recycled paper can save approximately 17 trees, 7,000 gallons of water, and enough energy to power an average home for six months.
Common types: printer paper, newspaper, cardstock, tissue paper, magazine paper
Strengths: lightweight, biodegradable, highly recyclable
Major concern: deforestation, water pollution from production process
Best practice: recycle whenever possible, use both sides of paper, choose recycled paper products
