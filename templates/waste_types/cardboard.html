Cardboard is a durable, versatile paper-based material commonly used for packaging, shipping containers, and various consumer products. Made from wood pulp fibers, cardboard is thicker and more rigid than standard paper, providing strength and protection while remaining relatively lightweight. As a biodegradable material, cardboard naturally breaks down over time when exposed to environmental elements. Recycling cardboard is highly efficient and beneficial, as it can be processed 5-7 times before the fibers become too short for further use. Each ton of recycled cardboard saves about 17 trees, 7,000 gallons of water, and 4,000 kilowatt-hours of electricity. When contaminated with food, oils, or other substances, cardboard's recyclability decreases, highlighting the importance of keeping cardboard clean and dry before recycling.
Common types: corrugated cardboard, paperboard, chipboard, boxboard, folding cartons
Strengths: lightweight yet strong, biodegradable, highly recyclable, cost-effective
Major concern: loses integrity when wet, can be contaminated by food or grease
Best practice: flatten before recycling, keep dry and clean, remove tape and labels when possible
