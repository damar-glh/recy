Metal waste consists of discarded items made from various metals, including aluminum, steel, copper, brass, and other metallic elements. Unlike many other waste materials, metals are highly recyclable and can be processed repeatedly without degrading their quality or properties. Mining and processing raw metals requires substantial energy and creates environmental impacts, making metal recycling particularly valuable. When recycled, metals save up to 95% of the energy required to produce them from virgin materials. Additionally, metal recycling reduces greenhouse gas emissions, conserves natural resources, and decreases the volume of waste in landfills. Metals do not degrade in landfills and can potentially leach harmful elements into soil and groundwater if not properly managed.
Common types: aluminum (cans, foil), steel, copper, brass, tin, iron
Strengths: highly durable, infinitely recyclable without quality loss, valuable commodities
Major concern: energy-intensive initial production, potential toxicity from some metal compounds
Best practice: separate from other waste streams, bring to specialized recycling facilities, clean before recycling
