Glass is a versatile, inert material made primarily from sand (silica), soda ash, and limestone. Commonly used for containers, windows, tableware, and decorative items, glass offers exceptional durability and doesn't degrade over time. Unlike many materials, glass can be recycled indefinitely without loss of quality or purity, making it an excellent candidate for closed-loop recycling systems. When properly recycled, glass helps conserve raw materials, reduces energy consumption, and decreases greenhouse gas emissions compared to producing new glass. However, when glass ends up in landfills, it remains there indefinitely since it doesn't biodegrade. Proper handling of glass waste is important as broken glass can pose safety hazards to waste management workers and contaminate other recyclable materials.
Common types: clear glass, green glass, brown glass, blue glass, tempered glass
Strengths: impermeable, chemically inert, infinitely recyclable without quality loss
Major concern: safety hazards when broken, heavy to transport, energy-intensive production
Best practice: rinse before recycling, separate by color when possible, reuse glass containers
